Special Projections

This is an implementation of the special projections described in the CERN Yellow Report "Event Display: can we see what we want?" from H. Drevermann, D. Kuhn and B.S. Nilsson, CERN/ECP 95-25. Some of the new technics are also described here.

The names of the variables, projections and and formulas used here are the same as in the report.

Remarks and Tips

 For most of these special projections only the markers representing the tracks should be visible. Lines in general should be avoided especially for the v-plot.

fish-eye

 In the xy view the circular fish eye transformation is used while along the z axis and for the rho'/z projection the rectangular fish eye transformation is chosen.

The fish-eye parameter a is also valid for the rho'/z projection!

The view is actually rescaled using rho_max and z_max. This radius and position along z remains fixed.

rho'/z

The parameter phi1 defines where the event is cut into two unconnected halves. Note that even single tracks might be cut into two pieces.
